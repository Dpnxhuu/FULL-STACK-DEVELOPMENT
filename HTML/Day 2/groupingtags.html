Breaking news from Mars Colony Alpha! Scientists have discovered a new type of crystal that glows in the dark. The team leader, Dr. Sarah Johnson, announced this finding on October 25th. The crystals, nicknamed "Starlight Stones," emit a bright blue glow when exposed to the cold Martian night. Researchers believe these crystals could revolutionize lighting systems in future space colonies.

These stones can also be used to create a new type of glass that is stronger and lighter than any other material. Further testing is needed however Dr. Sarah Johnson confirmed that their research was in the right direction.

This report was filed by James Wilson from Olympus Base. James has been reporting on Mars developments for the past 3 years.

In related news, the Luna Station has reported successful testing of their new gravity generators. The project, led by Dr. Maria Rodriguez, completed its final phase on 23rd. These developments mark a new chapter in human space exploration, with both Mars and Moon bases making significant progress in sustainable living conditions.

The gravity generators will allow the astronauts to build larger habitats and explore further into space.

Meanwhile, more equipment will have to be transported to the moon to support the new base. The company tasked with this project is already preparing for the upcoming mission.
